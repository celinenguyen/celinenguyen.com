What I've read this year / overall

Brief reviews of what I've read in 2020. This year, I'm particularly interested in reading nonfiction about: urbanism, the environment. For fiction: contemporary writers in the U.K., some more things to write here…

  1. Winter
    Ali Smith

    A luminous and joyful book, deeply rooted in contemporary British politics, art, and a reverence for nature. The second in Smith's seasonal quartet (but it stands beautifully alone).

  2. What Goes Up
    Michael Sorkin

    Sorkin is eloquent, entertainingly scathing (to the powerful), and empathetic (to the people). For those who love cities, public spaces, social housing, architecture, and Jane Jacobs.

  3. Kitchen
    Banana Yoshimoto

    A curious, wise, and infinitely kind novella on the tenderness of love, care, and losing loved ones. Also thrilling: an appreciation for food and explorations of gender roles.
