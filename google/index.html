I interned at Google this past summer, working on the Google Search App for iOS with a team of excellent designers, researchers, and engineers. (I'll share more screenshots and specifics about my work as the features I worked on get released!)

Design

The first month of my internship was spent designing a new onboarding flow, to match Google's new material design spec. I worked closely with other designers on our team, as well as some stakeholders in marketing, to develop a vibrant visual style that would clearly explain the important app features. The visual design I worked on heavily informed the animated onboarding flow that shipped to all users.

The rest of my internship was devoted to exploring higher-level questions about how users currently find information. I was able to work closely with other designers on my team, as well as the PM, tech lead, and engineers, to explore multiple interaction models for a core app feature. I produced a ton of mockups and flows (and a few interactive prototypes) to help communicate different models and their pros and cons to other members of the team.

Research

Throughout the summer, I got to assist in multiple user studies that the search UX researchers ran. At the end of the summer, I assisted in a comprehensive study to measure the effectiveness of various interaction models I'd worked on earlier in the summer. I wrote a study script, observed participants, and summarized the findings and provided concrete design suggestions to the team.
